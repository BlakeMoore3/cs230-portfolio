# cs230-portfolio

# Briefly summarize The Gaming Room client and their software requirements. Who was the client? What type of software did they want you to design?
The client was The Gaming Room, and they wanted to expand their game, Draw It or Lose It, so it could work across multiple operating systems. I designed a software solution that would support multiple players and game sessions while staying secure, reliable, and scalable as the game continued to grow.

# What did you do particularly well in developing this documentation?
I think I did a good job explaining the strengths and weaknesses of different operating platforms and using that information to make recommendations. I also made sure the design decisions were backed by the client's requirements instead of just personal preference.

# What about the process of working through a design document did you find helpful when developing the code?
Creating the design document helped me organize my thoughts before writing any code. It made it easier to understand what the application needed to do and gave me a clear plan to follow during development.

# If you could choose one part of your work on these documents to revise, what would you pick? How would you improve it?
If I could go back, I would spend more time expanding the networking and distributed systems sections. I understand those topics much better now and could provide more detailed explanations and examples.

# How did you interpret the user’s needs and implement them into your software design? Why is it so important to consider the user’s needs when designing?
I focused on the client's requirements, like supporting multiple users, preventing duplicate games, and making the application work across different platforms. Keeping the user's needs in mind is important because the software has to solve the client's problem, not just be technically correct.

# How did you approach designing software? What techniques or strategies would you use in the future to analyze and design a similar software application?
I started by understanding the requirements, then compared different platforms and technologies before making recommendations. In the future, I would continue using design documents early in the process because they help identify potential problems before development begins and make the implementation process much smoother.
